## What is Claude Opus 4.7?

Anthropic's flagship as of April 2026 — replacing Opus 4.6, sitting above Sonnet 4.6 and Haiku 4.5 in the price/performance ladder. 200K context, 128K max output, vision input, tool calling, and extended thinking. The pricing card up top is the headline number for most teams: **$5/M input, $25/M output**. That's roughly 5× the cost of GPT-5.5 on output, and ~12× the cost of Sonnet 4.6.

## Where it leads

- **SWE-Bench Pro (64.3%).** Best score we've seen on a mainstream API for the harder multi-language coding benchmark. Comfortably ahead of GPT-5.5 (58.6%) and Gemini 3.1 Pro (54.2%).
- **GPQA Diamond (94.2%).** Co-leader with Gemini 3.1 Pro at the saturation ceiling.
- **Tool calling under load.** Multi-step agentic flows where the model has to call 4–6 tools in sequence are where we saw Opus 4.7 pull furthest ahead of GPT-5.5 in actual production output, not just benchmark numbers.

## Where it doesn't

- **Pure speed.** No throughput benchmark in our catalog yet, but qualitatively it's not faster than GPT-5.5 on long generations.
- **Pricing.** $25/M output stings. A run that costs $0.30 on Sonnet 4.6 costs $1.50 on Opus 4.7.
- **Audio/video.** Vision only. If you need audio or video input, you're on Gemini 3.1 Pro or GPT-5.5.

## Who should pick what

| If you're doing... | We'd pick |
| --- | --- |
| Agentic coding, long debug loops, refactors across many files | **Claude Opus 4.7** |
| Casual chat, drafts, short emails | **Claude Sonnet 4.6** or **GPT-5 Mini** |
| Anything needing audio or video input | **Gemini 3.1 Pro** |
| Tight budget, willing to self-host | **DeepSeek V3.2** (open-weight) |

## Verdict

The first $5/$25 model we'd genuinely recommend without hedging — but only if you're doing the work that warrants it. For everything else, [Sonnet 4.6](/tools/claude-sonnet-4-6) is the answer.
